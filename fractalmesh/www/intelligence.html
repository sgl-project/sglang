Featured — April 2026

Resilience Intelligence Briefing

The world is entering a period where resilience is no longer a technical preference — it's a strategic necessity. Businesses face a convergence of pressures: rising compliance requirements, supply chain reconfiguration, geopolitical instability, energy volatility, and cloud dependency failures.

1. Geopolitical Instability Is Now a Top Operational Risk. Businesses that rely heavily on centralised cloud platforms or fragile digital workflows are feeling the pressure first. Resilience now begins with local control.

2. Supply Chains Are Being Redrawn. Manufacturers and logistics operators are moving toward local suppliers, local infrastructure, and local automation. Local systems fail less, cost less, and recover faster.

3. RF Intelligence Is Emerging as a New Verification Layer. Wireless environments are unique at every location — making them ideal for proof-of-presence, logistics validation, and compliance automation.
