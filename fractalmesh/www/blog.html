RF Intelligence

RF Fingerprinting Explained in Simple Terms

How wireless signals become privacy-preserving proof-of-presence — without collecting personal data.

April 2026 · Samuel James Hiotis

Every wireless environment has a unique signature — a fingerprint made up of the devices broadcasting, their signal strengths, and their patterns over time. RF fingerprinting captures this signature without identifying any individual device or person.

Used correctly, this creates tamper-evident proof-of-presence: you can prove a physical asset was in a specific location at a specific time, without installing tracking hardware or collecting personal data. This has enormous applications in logistics, compliance, and audit-ready supply chain management.

The GBWiGLE project uses exactly this approach — building local RF intelligence maps that serve as audit records without privacy violations. The data is held locally, processed locally, and stays sovereign.

Resilience

How to Build Infrastructure That Survives Cloud Outages

Hybrid Docker Compose + PM2 patterns for operational continuity when the cloud goes dark.

March 2026 · Samuel James Hiotis

The 2025 multi-cloud outages showed that resilience can't be achieved by adding more cloud. The answer is hybrid: keep critical data and processing local, use cloud only for what genuinely requires global distribution.

Pattern: run your primary database and core APIs on a local node. Mirror non-critical telemetry to cloud. Use Cloudflare tunnels for remote access without opening ports. Set PM2 to autorestart all critical processes with exponential backoff.

Result: when AWS goes down, your core operations continue. When your internet drops, your internal systems still work. When a vendor changes pricing, you're not hostage to it.

Compliance

How to Build Audit-Ready Systems

WAL-locked databases, immutable logs, lineage tracking — the architecture that makes compliance a structural property, not a checklist.

March 2026 · Samuel James Hiotis

Three structural properties make a system audit-ready: immutability (write-ahead logs you can't quietly edit), lineage (every record carries its provenance), and exportability (you can produce evidence on demand without scrambling).

Implementation: SQLite in WAL mode for all persistent data. Every significant operation logged to an append-only audit table with timestamp, actor, action, and hash of relevant data. Cron job exports weekly audit packs as signed CSV + JSON.

The regulator or auditor who shows up gets a dated, hashed evidence pack, not a frantic scramble through log files. That's the difference between structural compliance and compliance theatre.
