The AI will answer your question, and we'll show you which words it looked at while writing each word of its answer. This is called "attention" - it's literally where the AI pays attention.

Attention Zones ? What are Attention Zones?
We color-code each token based on where the AI was looking when it wrote that word. It's like a heat map of the AI's focus.

Why does this matter?
It reveals what the AI was "thinking about" - was it focused on nearby words (grammar) or looking far back (recalling information)?
Semantic Bridge Meaning: "Connecting ideas"
The AI is linking concepts from different parts of the text. Like when answering "What is the capital?" it connects "capital" to the country mentioned earlier.

When you see this: The AI is doing reasoning or retrieving related information.
Syntax Floor Meaning: "Following grammar"
The AI is focused on nearby words to get grammar right. Like making sure "the cat" is followed by "is" not "are".

When you see this: The AI is doing routine language construction, not deep thinking.
Exploration Meaning: "Unsure, exploring options"
The AI's attention is scattered - it's not sure what to write next and is considering multiple possibilities.

When you see this: Decision points, creative moments, or potential confusion.
Steering Meaning: "Following instructions"
The AI is looking way back at the original instructions or system prompt. It's checking what it was asked to do.

When you see this: The AI is staying on task, following your request.
